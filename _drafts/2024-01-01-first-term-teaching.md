I had planned on writing a short blog-post about Michalemas term 2023, my first experience of University teaching, but have let this slip back slowly into the new year. Perhaps that is enough proof in and of itself that it was a busy 8 weeks!

- when a student I found it difficult to avoid getting bogged down in the mechanics of answering specific problems, getting to the end of sheets etc.
- when teaching it was more important to focus on the bigger picture, and try and relate the questions to these ideas for the students
- it was hard work. working out how to do this did however help my own understanding, piecing together a mental map of the course and subject.
- i like lauren levines substack on ignorance - worth working through properly. makes me think of my random walk through the literature and not likig the mental map websites (not that I have actually ever used one)
- the idea of skills over information is interesting; and links to how I enjoy learning through research and exploring literature etc. - more natural way of building things together.
